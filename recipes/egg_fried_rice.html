Egg Fried Rice

This easy and customizable meal is my go to whenever I feel lazy, and have extra rice left from yesterday. Because it doesn't take too much time, and lets you hit those nutrient goals.

The version in this recipe is somewhat basic, but feel free to customize it by adding any protein (chicken, beef, etc.), or vegetables (corn, peas, green/red chili pepper, carrots, etc.) according to taste.

Inspiration: Uncle Roger

Servings:

3-4

Prep Time:

10 minutes

Active Time:

10 minutes

Total Time:

20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 4 tablespoons neutral oildivided
  • 4 eggslightly beaten
  • 4 scallions, thinly sliced crosswisedivided
  • 3-4 cups of cooked cold ricepreferrably day-old
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauceor to taste
  • Salt and ground black pepperto taste

Steps:

  1. Preheat a wok or a non-stick frying pan over high heat. Lower the heat to low or medium. Add 1 tablespoon of oil. Swirl to coat the surface of wok with oil.
  2. Add eggs. Cook and stir them until golden brown on the bottom for about 10-30 seconds. Flip eggs and cook them until golden brown on the other side.
  3. Return to high heat. Add 1 tablespoon of oil. Add white parts of scallions (and the rest of your veggies) on top of eggs, and stir-fry them until soft.
  4. Add the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil. Add rice, and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the rice is warm, breaking down eggs and clumps of rice with a spatula.
  5. Lower the heat to low. Add soy sauce, salt and ground black pepper to taste. Increase the heat to high and stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes until rice and eggs are evenly coated in the soy sauce, and rice doesn't have any large clumps.
  6. Taste and add more seasoning if needed. Turn the heat off and add green parts of scallions. Stir for another minute to mix everything together.
  7. Transfer egg fried rice to a plate or a bowl and serve hot. Enjoy!
